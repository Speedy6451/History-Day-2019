The Battle of Stalingrad

In August of 1942 the European Axis attacked Stalingrad in the Soviet Union. The strategically important city of Stalingrad guarded the oil that the axis desperately needed, the city falling would also be a huge morale boost, as Stalingrad bore the name of the soviet dictator, Joseph Stalin.
The Soviets broke the barrier of the relentless Axis advance at Stalingrad when troops under General Zhukov encircled and trapped the German sixth army and eventually defeated them. One can argue that this victory shortened the war by a considerable amount of time.

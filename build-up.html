The first year of the invasion of the Soviet Union had been succesful but it had not met its goal of pushing the soviets back past Moscow. The Axis turned south towards the oil in the caucuses which they needed and Stalingrad. The operation was named Fall Blau. If Fall Blau succeeded then the Germans would have oil and a propoganda and strategic victory, given that it was Stalin's namesake city. On June 28th, 1942 the German Army Group South attacked in a push towards the caucuses and Stalingrad, in just two weeks the German army had advanced 300 miles
